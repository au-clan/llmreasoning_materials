Answer: Kyle went to the store to buy glasses for his new apartment. Each glass costs $5, but every second glass is sold at 60% of the price. He wants to buy 16 glasses.

To determine the total cost:
1. Since every second glass is discounted, for 16 glasses, half will be full price and half will be discounted.
2. Number of full-price glasses: \( \frac{16}{2} = 8 \)
3. Number of discounted glasses: \( \frac{16}{2} = 8 \)
4. Cost of full-price glasses: \( 8 \times 5 = 40 \) dollars
5. Cost of discounted glasses: \( 8 \times (5 \times 0.6) = 8 \times 3 = 24 \) dollars
6. Total cost: \( 40 + 24 = 64 \) dollars

Thus, Kyle should pay \(\boxed{64}\) dollars.
